Strong demand for HKIA appeases railway link and bridge challenges

Reporter: Jimmy Ding

The economic benefits brought by the third runway were not split by connections built between Hong Kong and mainland China.

Dr. Law Cheung-kwok, director of aviation policy and research center in Chinese University of Hong Kong said the building of Guangzhou-Shenzhen-Hong Kong Express Rail Link (XRL) and Hong Kong-Zhuhai-Macao Bridge will not deeply affect the economic benefits brought by the third runway since the aviation sector in Delta River Area as a whole has huge potential.

Law said he is not worried about the economic benefits brought by the third runway, "The growth of airline passenger globally is around 3% to 4%, the growth for Asia is 5% to 6%, while the Delta River Area is around 7% to 8% per year. Though the third runway is under building phase, in the long term, it will serve the demand," said Law.

"Hong Kong-Zhuhai-Macao Bridge is more convenient for tourists in Greater Bay Area to transfer in Hong Kong which add more passengers to the Hong Kong International Airport," said Peng Lu, assistant professor in Aeronautical and aviation engineering Hong Kong Polytechnic University.

Hong Kong-Zhuhai-Maocao Bridge was designed to connect three economic heavy-weights that each have different social systems.

Although experts are not concerned Hong Kong-Zhuhai-Macao Bridge or XRL with mainland China may carry off HKIA's market share, some people are heading to mainland airports or railway stations by these transportation means.

Louise Yuan, a student in Hong Kong who comes from Wuhan said "XRL really changed my transportation habit. Now I take the XRL to go back to Wuhan but before the building of XRL, I always take airplane from Hong Kong to Wuhan."

Although the High-Speed Rail may grab market share of the Hong Kong airport. HKIA stated in the 2030 master plan, saying regional Mainland routes combined contributed only about 3% of HKIA's passenger throughput in 2010. Therefore, any negative impact from XRL would unlikely be significant.

The HKIA said that building of Air bridges between Hong Kong and the Mainland is the right way to attract new sources of flow to HKIA of both people and goods, and to increase economic activities in two regions. Air bridges will allow travelers to go to a city center in the PRC and check in for domestic and international flights.

"Every time I go home, I bought air ticket in Shenzhen, it is much cheaper than go back home from Hong Kong," said Bob Han, another student study in Hong Kong. Han was from Shandong province in mainland China.

"Actually, neither does XRL nor Hong Kong-Zhuhai-Macao Bridge affect my decision for buying air ticket in Shenzhen. I do not care about these new constructions. I always take MTR to Shenzhen and from there I take Shenzhen subway to go to the airport," said Han.

Guangzhou–Shenzhen–Hong Kong Express Rail Link connects Hong Kong to other cities in mainland China

Han said since Hong Kong has many holidays for its students. He usually travels to different cities in mainland China. He said "If it is travelling in the mainland, definitely buying ticket from Shenzhen, the reason is the same, much cheaper."

There are many students who come from mainland and study in Hong Kong just like Han, and most of them choose to buy air ticket in Shenzhen and Guangzhou to go back to their hometowns or travel in mainland cities.

Han said, "many of my classmates choose to go back to their hometowns by taking airplanes from Guangzhou and Shenzhen, some of them really appreciate the XRL as the air ticket from Guangzhou is even cheaper than from Shenzhen."

Rings Tse, a Hong Kong student whose grandparents live in Shunde said "I feel the XRL saves a lot of time if I go back to visit my grandparents. However, if I am going to mainland cities for travelling, I still prefer Hong Kong as departure."

Rings explained he cared more about convenience rather than price. "The price difference from Shenzhen and Hong Kong maybe around 300-500 RMB but convenience is more important for me and I do not trust the security system in mainland China."

The construction of XRL and Hong Kong-Zhuhai-Macao bridge may affect people's decision on travelling to mainland cities. They may take railway to go back their hometowns instead of taking airplanes. But for people from Shanghai and Beijing, it does not matter that much.


Selina Liu is from Shanghai and currently working in Hong Kong. "I take airplanes from Hong Kong airport every time I go back home." Liu said.

She said the air ticket from Hong Kong to Shanghai is not expensive probably because there are many flights flied between Shanghai and Hong Kong. The price of air ticket from Hong Kong to Shanghai and Beijing is relative cheaper comparing to other small cities in mainland China since Hong Kong has many flights flying to Beijing and Shanghai.

Law said in the long term, both XRL and Hong Kong-Zhuhai-Macau Bridge would meet the growing demands under the Greater Bay Area policy. However, HKIA would not forfeit its status as the function of international transfer hub is still there.
